Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar

About

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar (14 April 1891 â€“ 6 December 1956), also known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was an Indian jurist, economist, politician and social reformer. He was the principal architect of the Constitution of India and a champion for the rights of the Dalits and other socially backward classes.

Achievements

  • First Law Minister of Independent India
  • Chairman of the Constitution Drafting Committee
  • Fought against untouchability and social discrimination
  • Founded the Independent Labour Party and later the Scheduled Castes Federation
  • Converted to Buddhism in 1956, inspiring a mass movement among Dalits

Legacy

Dr. Ambedkar's work laid the foundation for social justice in India. His ideas continue to inspire millions and his birthday, April 14, is celebrated as Ambedkar Jayanti across the country.
